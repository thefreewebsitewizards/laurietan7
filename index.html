Writing

Crafting compelling narratives that explore the human experience through diverse perspectives.

Laurie's plays have been read or produced locally at Broward College, Femuscripts, Thinking Cap Theatre, NCPLab, Naked Angels, Cutler Bay Community Theatre, Nova Theatre (Ann Arbor, Michigan), PlayZoomers, and at the Voices of Women Theatre Festival in Tampa, New City Players in Ft. Lauderdale, and at Miami One-Acts.

Publications

  • New Father, a monologue, Smith and Kraus
  • Sex Ed for Mom, Solis Press
  • NewPlayExchange profile

Laurie recently directed and adapted the screenplay of the short film, Stubborn Grace, currently on the film festival circuit, co-produced by Project Soar and Andronache Studios.

Acting

Bringing characters to life with depth and authenticity on stage and screen.

Most recently Laurie was seen as Momo in The Humans at Palm Beach Dramaworks, Violet Jenkins in Second Chance by Mike Vogel, and Claribel Cone in The Equivalent of Sensation by Arianna Rose.

She is a regular actor on the YouTube series, Soul Snack. Co-founder of Femuscripts Productions, Laurie played roles, directed and wrote plays for their Long Story Shorts, Love Fest, LoveFest2, Lovely Light Readings, and Florida: Her Stories play festivals.

Laurie starred in the award-winning Stubborn Grace. Other recent credits include Mistress Overdone/the Prioress in Palm Beach Shakespeare's Measure for Measure.

Currently, Laurie is preparing for her debut in stand-up, and will be featured at various locations in South Florida.

Directing

Shaping creative visions and guiding productions from concept to completion.

Laurie turned to directing when she co-founded Femuscripts, a theatre company dedicated to producing works by women. She directed two films in 2024, Stubborn Grace, an award-winning short film made in collaboration with Project Soar and Andronache Studios, and Trippin' in Town Hall, a film made for the 48-Hour Film Project, Miami-Ft. Lauderdale version.

Her latest short film, Athena's Wisdom, will be released shortly. On stage, she directed a staged reading of the works written during her playwriting workshop for Femuscripts Productions, The Love Space Demands.

Laurie has twice directed for Miami One-Acts, a popular bi-annual short play festival in Miami. She directed The Kenmore Hotel in 2024, several plays between 2020 and 2023, including Dark Matter by Donald Loftus and her own play Sex Ed for Mom with Play Zoomers.

In 2020, she founded Femuscripts Productions, which produced several online play festivals, and debuted with a live festival in May 2022, LoveFest 2. Laurie directed six of thirteen plays in LoveFest 2.
